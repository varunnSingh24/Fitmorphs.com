Diabetes Reversal

Can Pre-Diabetes Be Reversed

If your HbA1c is between 5.7 and 6.4, you are still standing on the only patch of metabolic ground where full reversal is almost certain. Here is what to do, and what not to do.

Reviewed by Dr. Abhishek Mane, MBBS MD Chief Medical Officer Published 2026-05-12 6 min read Last reviewed 2026-05-18

A diagnosis of prediabetes means your blood sugar levels are higher than normal, but not yet high enough to be classified as Type 2 diabetes. The good news? The answer to the question "can prediabetes be reversed completely?" is a resounding yes.

The Window of Opportunity

Prediabetes is a critical crossroads. Left unmanaged, up to 70% of individuals with prediabetes will eventually develop full-blown Type 2 diabetes. However, because pancreatic beta-cell function is largely intact at this stage, the condition is highly responsive to lifestyle interventions.

How to Reverse Prediabetes

1. Shed Visceral Fat

You don't need to lose massive amounts of weight to see results. Studies show that losing just 5% to 7% of your body weight (specifically the visceral fat around your abdomen) can restore normal insulin sensitivity and completely halt the progression to diabetes.

2. Build Muscle Mass

As previously mentioned, muscles are glucose sinks. Hypertrophy (muscle building) training expands the capacity of your muscles to store glycogen. The more muscle you have, the lower your fasting blood sugar will be.

3. Cut the Sugar Taps

If the tub is overflowing, you don't just grab a mop; you turn off the tap. Reducing dietary sugars and refined carbohydrates gives your pancreas the desperately needed break it requires to heal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my prediabetes is reversed?
Prediabetes is considered reversed when your fasting blood sugar falls below 100 mg/dL and your HbA1c stays consistently below 5.7% without the use of medication.
Will I have to eat a strict diet forever?
The strict phase is temporary. Once you regain metabolic flexibility and clear fat from your liver, you can transition to an 80/20 lifestyle where occasional treats don't derail your health.
